## Answers

### Question 1: Active Listening (Minimum 6 points)
1. Stop unnecessary thoughts and focus completely on the speaker.
2. Maintain eye contact to show attention and interest.
3. Avoid planning a response while the other person is still speaking.
4. Do not get distracted by devices like phones or laptops.
5. Ignore surrounding noise or people and stay mentally present.
6. Listen patiently without interrupting or changing the topic.
7. Show listening through nodding or small acknowledgements.
8. Ask questions if something is unclear to confirm understanding.
9. Summarize what the speaker said to ensure correct understanding.

---

### Question 2: Fisher’s Model - Reflective Listening (Key Points)
1. Listen carefully without interrupting.
2. Understand both the speaker’s message and emotions.
3. Reflect back what was understood in simple words.
4. Clarify meaning by asking questions when needed.
5. Show the speaker that they are being understood.
6. Encourage the speaker to continue sharing.
7. Avoid judging, criticizing, or correcting too quickly.

---

### Question 3: Obstacles in My Listening Process
- I sometimes get distracted by my surroundings.
- My mind often gets stuck on one point the speaker said, and I start overthinking it.
- If I feel the topic is not useful, I lose interest and stop paying attention.

---

### Question 4: What Can I Do to Improve My Listening?
- I should consciously focus only on the speaker and their message.
- I should maintain eye contact and use head nods to show I am engaged.
- I should avoid using devices during conversations.
- I should stop overthinking and bring my attention back to what the person is saying.
- I should listen with patience even if the topic feels unimportant.
- I should summarize important points to confirm I understood correctly.

---

### Question 5: When Do You Switch to Passive Communication?
I become passive when I am surrounded by unknown people who seem intimidating. I also become passive with people I know well when I want to avoid unnecessary conflict and protect the relationship, unless the issue starts causing serious trouble for me.

---

### Question 6: When Do You Switch into Aggressive Communication?
I feel aggressive when people irritate me, repeat the same mistakes again and again, or try to act tough. However, I usually control myself and do not behave aggressively because most people around me do not act aggressively either.

---

### Question 7: When Do You Switch into Passive Aggressive Communication?
I sometimes become passive aggressive with friends, mainly through sarcasm or indirect comments. I usually do it in a playful way to build a closer bond and make the friendship more comfortable. However, it can be risky because sarcasm may be misunderstood, so I still try to keep it within limits and make sure I do not hurt anyone seriously.

---

### Question 8: How Can You Make Your Communication Assertive?
- I maintain eye contact and speak confidently.
- I explain my point clearly and directly.
- I use "I" statements like "I feel", "I expect", or "I need" instead of blaming.
- I clearly state what must be done and what should not be done.
- I set boundaries and communicate expectations properly.
- I stay calm and avoid being emotional or rude.
- I warn people firmly when necessary, without insulting them.
